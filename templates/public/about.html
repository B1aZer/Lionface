About Lionface
There are three primary goals of Lionface: maintain an environment free of visual clutter (specifically in regards to advertisements), protect and respect people's privacy down to the smallest detail, and contribute to the community in a meaningful and positive way.

Lionface is built in Python.

Degree of Separation

Degree of Separation represents the number of people that separate you from any given person on Lionface. For example, the Degree of Separation between you and your friend is 0 because you know them directly. The Degree of Separation between you and your friend's friend is 1, because your fried stands between you and them. Your friend's friend's friend and you have a Degree of Separation of 2, and so on.

Albums

Albums on Lionface are more comprehensive than traditional. Instead of just compiling images into Albums, say from a trip to Germany, you can attach multiple photos (and videos) to a post, write what you want, and then compile those posts into Albums. You could have a post for your first day in Germany, with photos and videos, and then a post from your visit to Hiedelberg Castle in another post - attach these posts to your Germany Album, and the presentation and organization becomes much cleaner and more efficient. Or, have an Album just for your poetry, or favorite websites.

Lionface does not currently support videos directly. Videos can be embedded from sites like Youtube and Vimeo.

Posts

Posting on Lionface is pretty typical, but there are a few new features. First, you can add tags to posts (and Follow a person's public posts), similar to Twitter. You can embed videos from sites like Youtube and Vimeo, or TED.com. You can also use standard HTML to create links within your post (see an HTML tutorial for details).

Posts can also be attached to albums, as we said above. Post can contain multiple photos. We also hope you'll enjoy the initmate control you'll have over your posts via the privacy settings.
